# Less Apparatus, More Chart

*A short follow-up draft for Gears of Resistance / mbparks.com*

BAROGRAPH v1.4.0 takes the drum off the drum. The faux cylinder, the brass pillars, the spindle, the animated pen arm: retired. What remains is what mattered all along, seven days of weather in seven inks on aged chart paper, with bold scale numerals printed in each trace's color and a day of blank ruled chart waiting ahead of the present.

Skeuomorphism is a spice, and I had been cooking with rather a lot of it. The curved paper was charming, but it bent every line on the chart in service of an illusion, and an instrument should serve the reading, not the theater. The type, the inks, the ruling, and the gaslight stay; those are material, not costume.

The flat sheet also bought a genuinely useful thing: rest your pointer anywhere on the chart and a hairline crosshair snaps to the nearest observation, rings each active trace in its own ink, and reads out the time and values on a small card. The pen used to rest only on the present moment. Now your finger can rest on any moment of the week.
